Principal Claims — Ref. Disclosure DSL-2026-001
CLAIM 1A spherical periodic table wherein radial distance from center corresponds to environmental pressure and temperature regimes of planetary layers.
CLAIM 2Application of period/group longitude/latitude coordinate grid to a spherical periodic table preserving backward compatibility with standard notation.
CLAIM 3Assignment of back hemisphere (lon. 180°–360°) to antimatter elements at mirrored period/group coordinates.
CLAIM 4Designation of equatorial shell exterior to atmospheric layer for dark matter entry De (Delirium), Z=?, ø-Block classification.
CLAIM 5Placement of H (Z=1) at north pole as elemental origin and Z=137 (Feynmanium) at south pole as physics boundary.
CLAIM 6Spectral emission frequency as a formal periodic property; Delirium frequency (λ=?) designated as primary dark matter detection target.
