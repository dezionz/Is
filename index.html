Theoretical Boundary Note — Z=137 / Z=173
† Z=137 anchors the south pole as the classical relativistic limit under a point-nucleus model: at this atomic number the 1s electron would require orbital velocity ≥ c, encoding the fine structure constant α⁻¹ ≈ 137 as a hard seam in spacetime.   The more rigorous limit under extended nuclear charge (QED) treatment is Z=173, beyond which spontaneous electron-positron pair production from the vacuum makes stable atomic structure impossible. Elements Z=138–173, if synthesized, would extend this table's south polar region. Z > 173 is considered physically impossible under current quantum electrodynamic models — requiring a new table framework.
